What do we do?

Regulation and oversight

Setting production quotas and pricing mechanisms, enforcing quality standards and food safety regulations, and regulating the activities of mills and refineries.

Research and development

Funding and supporting research on improving sugar cane cultivation, processing techniques, and product development. Promoting the adoption of new technologies and best practices within the industry.

Industry representation and advocacy

Representing the interests of the sugar industry to policymakers and other stakeholders. Promoting the economic and social importance of the sugar industry at the national and international level.

Process of Extracting Sugar from Sugar Cane

Harvesting: Sugarcane is manually or mechanically harvested, when the sugar content is highest. The harvested cane is transported to the sugar mill.
Preparation: The sugarcane is washed to remove any dirt or debris. The cane is then cut into smaller pieces or shredded to increase the surface area for the extraction process.
Extraction: The prepared cane is fed into a mill, which consists of a series of heavy rollers that crush the cane and extract the sugarcane juice. The sugarcane juice is collected.
Clarification: The extracted sugarcane juice is heated with lime to raise the pH, allowing impurities like wax, gums, and fibers to settle and be removed.
Evaporation: The clarified juice is then concentrated by evaporation, which removes excess water and increases the sugar concentration. This is typically done in a series of evaporators, where the juice is heated under reduced pressure to lower the boiling point and increase the efficiency of the evaporation process.
Crystallization: The concentrated syrup, now called "molasses," is further processed in a vacuum pan to facilitate the formation of sugar crystals. The sugar crystals are then separated from the remaining molasses through a centrifugal process.
Drying and Packaging: The separated sugar crystals are then dried and stored. Finally, the refined sugar is packaged and ready for distribution and consumption.
